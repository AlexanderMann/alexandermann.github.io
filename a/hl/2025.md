# 2025 Holiday Letter

Natalie and I have been debating about how to start this letter. The start is, in fact, the hardest part. The mechanics of the middle are dead simple: headers for each event (or family member), fill those in, toss in some photos (and in recent years include "reject" (read: Alex approved, but not Natalie approved) photos at the end). But maybe this is apt, because for us, the start of 2025 was difficult.

It began with great loss and great joy. At 1am, after ringing in the New Year with smooches, friends, and a Beatles singalong, we got a phone call from our friend and neighbour [Kim](https://www.washingtonpost.com/health/2025/06/16/terminal-cancer-diagnosis-palliative-care/). We knew immediately what this call meant. Her husband, [Alec](https://www.legacy.com/us/obituaries/legacyremembers/alec-dubro-obituary?id=57314922), had passed away. Thirty seconds later, and we were sitting together sharing stories, waiting for the rabbi to arrive, and taking in a moment that taught us so much about life and love. It was easily one of the greatest honours anyone could ever bestow upon us. A weird beginning in an ending, but loving, joyous, and awe-inspiring nonetheless.

![Kim and Alec](../../img/2025/kim-and-alec.jpg)

Seven months later, and [Kim](https://www.washingtonpost.com/obituaries/2025/07/25/kim-fellner/) had also passed. She was surrounded by the most loving family anyone could ever ask for and in the sanctity of her own home. Kim and Alec were both truly extraordinary people. You should do yourself the favour of reading both of their obit's (Kim, Alec), and especially Kim's sendoff series in the [Washington Post: Living with Dying](https://www.washingtonpost.com/health/2025/06/16/terminal-cancer-diagnosis-palliative-care/).

> Intimacy is not accidental

When we moved to DC in 2023, Kim greeted us and got us on the listserv. She was the matriarch of the block. Since I was in the middle of practicing two cake recipes for our Second Wedding, I was able to send her off with slices in tow. Thankfully, they were good slices, because a quick, easy, and deep friendship ensued. In one of her Washpo op-eds about carjacking in DC, she took a brief aside to dub me the "baking ambassador" (a title that puts me into a long line of ambassadors on Natalie's side of the family). We lived at 522; Kim and Alec lived at 521‚Äìwe frequently waved to each other from our living rooms. Still to this day, we're humbled at how they spent the time to build such a beautiful friendship with us when they had such a limited amount to spend.

Soon after Kim's funeral, we spoke with her family about the possibility of buying Kim and Alec‚Äôs home. A month later, we had closed on 521. I'll leave the update on 521 (a new header in the mechanical part of the letter) for later, but suffice it to say, we have big shoes to fill (Kim was not even 5' ironically) and have been excited by the challenge. We love our neighbourhood and our community. Our sincere hope is that everyone finds their block matriarch, baking ambassador, and community, and works on building something together, instead of tearing things down alone.

And, with that, 2025...

## Halloween: Cone Heads

![Beam me up](../../img/2025/flying-saucer-astronaut.jpg)

We were fully moved in on October 1st. Not wanting to let down our public, Alex quickly got to work on building just what he'd threatened to do the whole year: a full size flying saucer, suspended in midair. Natalie provided our costumes (sadly not one for Jules) and the Cone Heads had returned to planet Earth. We handed out around 600 pieces of candy this year, and we got lots of fun reactions to the decorations.

![Cone Heads and the Terrifier](../../img/2025/cone-heads.jpg)

One little boy was dressed as [Goku](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/eMAtSpOy3sY), and had picked his hair out to be in the shape of a Sayan. He walked up, stood in front of the flying saucer, and then made his parents film him as he told the camera how the Sayans had invaded Earth, and he must stop them.

One of our new neighbours (moved to the block in August/September), was telling Alex in early October about how the decorations in the broader neighbourhood were really impressive, and how there was good competition on the block. Alex simply responded "no." Thank god Alex now has such a healthy outlet like a house to work on instead of planning out what to do for next year's Halloween (designs are already in the works).
